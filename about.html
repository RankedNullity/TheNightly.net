Developer, Mathematician, Entrepeneur

Hello, I'm Jaron Wang, the creator of this website. I was born in Beijing, China, and moved to the United States when I was just entering grade school, so about 8 years old. I've been around most of the west coast, having lived in most parts of California, Oregon, and Washington.

Education

I went to high school at Redmond High School (Washington) and with the help of some of the most wonderful teachers I've ever met, I found my passion for all things science and technology. And if that sounds broad, that's because it is. I took AP class after AP class, hoping to narrow down my passions; instead, I only added to the list. By the time I graduated, I was left with more questions than answers.

The fall after graduation, I started attending the University of Washington with my ego a little too bloated from high school. That quarter, I took 22 credits while working full time as a sous chef. Needless to say, my hubris took its toll. As crazy as I am, it was still a little much, even for me. Following that, I decided to take it easy and take some intro-level courses, so I could find my true interests. Then, I heard this quote somewhere.

"If you ever have to make a decision, flip a coin. Bet on the outcome, and if you regret the decision you made, then you should chose the other option."

Which is essentially what I did. The whole time I was taking other classes, I just felt myself craving for a math class. So that was eventually what I decided on. Now, I'm midway through my third year double majoring in Applied and Compuational Mathematical Sciences (ACMS) and Theoretical Mathematics with a minor in Physics, and I quite like it. Currently, I'm taking:

  • MATH462: Combinatorial Theory II
  • MATH327: Introductory Real Analysis I
  • STAT390: Statistical Methods in Engineering and Science
  • ESS102: Space and Space Travel

Interests

I always have a hard time distinguishing between "Hobbies" and "Interests". To me, a hobby is something you do in your free time, and interests are something you take more seriously. This never made sense to me, because I have fun when with my interests, and I take my hobbies seriously. So I guess for the purposes of splitting them, I shall use a societal perspetive when determing the label "Hobby" or "Interest".
